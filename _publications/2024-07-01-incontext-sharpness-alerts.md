This work introduces **in-context sharpness** as a signal extracted from the inner representations of large language models (LLMs) to identify and mitigate hallucinations. By leveraging the sharpness of in-context representations, we can detect untruthful outputs at generation time, enabling more reliable and truthful LLM outputs. The approach is validated on multiple hallucination benchmarks and shows consistent improvements.

**Authors**: Shiqi Chen, Miao Xiong, Junteng Liu, Zhengxuan Wu, Teng Xiao, Siyang Gao, Junxian He
